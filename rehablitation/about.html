About Us

Yahoo Rehab is a premier rehabilitation facility where clients are treated with care and concern. It was established in 2002, as a 12-bed facility. Today, it has grown to 30 beds for men and women housed in two facilities. Yahoo Rehab has earned an international reputation for its commitment, ethical standards and professional approach toward effective treatment of addictions.

In a country with few treatment facilities and a lack of guidelines and prescribed standards, Yahoo Rehab has pioneered and benchmarked standards and protocols.

Yahoo Rehab has been rated among the TOP 4 rehabs in India by Reader’s Digest (July 2007). Yahoo Rehab’s model is based on the famed ‘Minnesota Model’ initiated in USA. The treatment aims at total abstinence achieved through the Twelve Steps program. The 12-Steps treatment model has been approved by WHO (World Health Organization) as the ‘most effective treatment for addictions’.

Staff

A substantial number of the therapy team consists of recovering alcoholics and addicts: those who have travelled the road, experienced the pain of addiction and the joys of recovery. This lends an unmatched touch of empathy and identification to the therapy process

Hope Trust ensures its staff is up-to-date and highly motivated. The staff members attend regular in-house continued skills-development programs. Several members are enrolled in online educational programs, leading to international certification by IC&RC (International Certification & Reciprocity Consortium) and CACCF (Canadian Addiction Counselors Certification Federation).

Programs

Hope Trust is collaborating with the world-renowned Center for Cellular & Molecular Biology (CCMB) for Research Programs on the generic basis of addiction, through DNA analysis of ADH, ALDH, and DRD2 genes.

Moreover, Hope Trust collects, collates and analyses extensive data for research on the bio-psycho-socio aspects of addiction.

Commitment

Hope Trust is concerned and committed to the cause of addictions in the community at large and conducts regular awareness programs in schools, colleges and clubs and frequently interacts with the media to sensitize various sections of the society.
